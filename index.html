Robert 'Bob' Nester Marley

The King of Reggae

Here's a time line of Bob Marley's life:

  • February 6, 1945: Bob Marley is born in Nine Mile, Saint Ann Parish, Jamaica.
  • 1957: Marley moves to Kingston to live with his mother and begins his music career.
  • 1963: Bob Marley and The Wailers release their first album, "Catch a Fire."
  • 1972: Marley signs with Island Records and releases the album "Burning" with The Wailers.
  • 1974: The Wailers release the album "Natty Dread," featuring the hit "No Woman, No Cry."
  • 1976: Marley survives an assassination attempt just days before a concert for peace in Jamaica.
  • 1977: Bob Marley and The Wailers release the album "Exodus," which includes hits like "Jamming" and "Three Little Birds."
  • 1980: Marley performs at the Amandla Festival in Boston, his last concert.
  • May 11, 1981: Bob Marley dies of cancer in Miami at the age of 36.
